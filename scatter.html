Scatter plots can also be used to find correlations between data. These could be positive correlations, or negative correlations. If the plotted points pattern slopes from lower left to upper right, this means that the value of X increases as the value of Y increase (as is in the graph above), suggesting a positive correlation. If the pattern of plotted points slopes from upper left to lower right, the values of X increase as the values of Y decrease, suggesting a negative correlation. Some scatter plots, though, might not show any signs of positive or negative correlations.
